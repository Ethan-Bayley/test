General Info

WELCOME to the VEX ROBOTICS 2024-2025 AUSTRALIAN NATIONAL CHAMPIONSHIPS!!

 

This year teams will need to qualify from their State Championships to win a spot to the National Championships.

The Australian National Championships will be held from Wednesday the 4th of December to Friday the 6th of December at Rose Hill Racecourse in Sydney NSW.

Robot Inspection/Check and the Girl Powered Workshop in will be on Wednesday the 4th of December.

Competition days will be Thursday the 5th and Friday the 6th of December 2024.

- Each Person will need a  Humanitix ticket to enter the event

 

Teams who have qualified to the Australian National Championships will need to have their Engineering Notebook in Digital format, the LINK to the Digital Engineering Notebook must be uploaded by November the 13th 2024 by 5pm. New this year, all teams aiming for a judged award need to have submitted an engineering notebook. *

Once the link is uploaded via Robotevents.com, teams may still update their DEN on an ongoing basis. Engineering Notebook content is expected to change over time, which is part of the Engineering Design Process. Judges will access the links on Event Days and will be given the link provided on November the 13th. For those uploading PDF images who are concerned the timestamp will change, you can link a file , please make sure that each update only includes the most up to date Engineering Notebook so there is no confusion for our Volunteer Judges.

Digital Engineering Notebooks should be freely accessible by the judges. Please make sure your permissions are set to allow judges to view your links with no passwords

You can find more information regarding the Judging process in the Guide to Judging.  Notebooks will not be accepted at the event.

Teams must complete their Participant Release Form , prior to competiting. 

Rose Hill Racecourse: Is a cashless venue. There will be food available for purchase on site. Please makesure to bring a waterbottle and stay hydrated. 

Volunteers: are always welcome! THIS EVENT IS NOT POSSIBLE WITHOUT VOLUNTEERS
This year volunteers will recieve online training, catering, an event shirt and a Fast Pass at the end of their shift. Without the help of our amazing community of volunteers, this event is not possible. If you have a volunteer to nominate please fill out this Volunteer formor email [email protected]

 

Girl Powered Workshop: If you are interested in attending our Girl Powered Workshop, please register by emailing Chris_Harding@ RECF.org.

Code of Conduct

The REC Foundation considers the positive, respectful, and ethical conduct of teams to be an essential component of the competition. Participants are expected to behave in a respectful and professional manner, and to operate as student-centered teams with limited adult assistance. This includes all students, teachers, coaches, mentors, parents, and spectators associated with a team.

For more details, please refer to the REC Foundation Code of Conduct and Student-Centered Policy. If you have concerns about compliance with the Code of Conduct, please speak to the Event Partner during the event.

Volunteer

This event is not possible without Volunteers. 

Volunteers will be provided with online training and will have dedicated team leaders. Volunteers will be provided catering, an event shirt and a Fast Pass (to use on the Skills Field ) 

Volunteer roles include: 

Field Resetters -rest the fields after play

Queuing Team - maintain the match schedule by coralling teams as they line up in the clearly marked waiting bays.

Score Keepers - data input using tables provided by VEX. Tablets use an image based platform to input the scores. All scorekeepers will have the support of a certified Head Ref

Judges - Judges will be needed from November the 15th 2024 to remotely access engineering notebooks via their own personal devices from the comfort of their own homes and mark engineering notebooks using a standard, publicly available rubric. On site from December 5th-6th 2024, Judges will be needed to conduct interviews with teams using a standard, publicly available rubric.

If you are intersted in Volunteering please fill out this Volunteer form, or email [email protected]

Emergency/Bad Weather Policy

Should the weather be deamed unsafe, the event will be canceled. 

As per the Qualifying Criteria, should the National Championships need to be canceled, teams will be selected from the World Skills Standings to represent Australia at the World Championships in 2025.

Refund and Payment Policies

If the Event Partner cancels the event: 

If the event is canceled due to Weather or Covid-19, teams will not recieve a refund.

If a Coach/Mentor completes registration for the event then chooses to withdraw or not attend:

Should a team wish to withdraw, they will need to request this via email to [email protected] by COB November the 21st 2024. No refunds will made avialable after November the 21st 2024.

Age Information

V5RC - 

Student – A person is considered a Student if they meet both of the following criteria: 

1. Anyone who is earning or has earned credit toward a secondary school (i.e., high school) diploma, certificate, or other equivalent during the six (6) months preceding the VEX Robotics World Championship. Courses earning credits leading up to high school would satisfy this requirement.

 2. Anyone born after May 1, 2005 (i.e., who will be 19 or younger at VEX Worlds 2025). Eligibility may also be granted based on a disability that has delayed education by at least one year.

 • Middle School Student – A Student born after May 1, 2009 (i.e., who will be 15 or younger at VEX Worlds 2025). Any Students who meet this criteria may also compete as High School Students.

 • High School Student – Any eligible Student that is not a Middle School Student.
